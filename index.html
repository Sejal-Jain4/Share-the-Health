Background

Health disparities are referred to as preventable differences in medical conditions. These disparities disproportianately impact those of minority backgrounds, who often receive inadequate treatment compared to white populations. This difference in healthcare leads to an imbalance in the quality of treatment for minorities. They can then face worsening health conditions which places racial minorities at a significant disadvantage.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a health disparity?

    Health disparities are differences in health and medical conditions. For example smokers have greater risk of heart disease compared to nonsmokers.

  • How does race impact health disparities?

    Data proves that minorites face greater illness including diabetes, obesity, and asthma.

  • Why is it important to recognize racial health disparities in society?

    Recognizing that these differences exist will help us change our racial biases and actually work towards decreasing racial health disparities. We need to analyze the choices that influence greater mortality rates, premature birth rates, etc. in minorites in order to create equal medical care for all races.
